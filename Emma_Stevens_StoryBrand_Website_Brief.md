## PART 1: THE BRANDSCRIPT

---

### 1. CHARACTER (The Customer)

**Who is the hero?**
A small business owner in Hertfordshire or Buckinghamshire — typically a sole trader, partnership owner, or owner-manager of a limited company. They're good at what they do, but the financial admin side of running a business drains their time and energy.

**What is the ONE thing they want?**
To have their business finances handled properly so they can stop worrying about tax, paperwork, and HMRC — and get back to running their business.

**Want statement (in the customer's own words):**
"I want an accountant I can actually talk to — someone who takes the paperwork off my plate, keeps me on the right side of HMRC, and helps me keep more of what I earn."

---

### 2. PROBLEM

**The Villain:**
The complexity and confusion of UK tax and accounting compliance. It's an ever-changing, jargon-filled system designed for accountants, not business owners — and it punishes people who fall behind.

**External Problem (the tangible issue):**
"I've got tax returns to file, accounts to prepare, VAT to deal with — and I don't have the time or knowledge to do it properly."

**Internal Problem (how it makes them feel):**
"I feel overwhelmed and anxious. I lie awake worrying that I'm missing a deadline, paying too much tax, or that HMRC is going to come knocking. I'm embarrassed that my paperwork is in a mess."

**Philosophical Problem (the injustice):**
"Small business owners who work hard to build something shouldn't have to lose sleep over paperwork and tax."

---

### 3. GUIDE (Emma Stevens Accountancy)

**Empathy:**
Emma understands what it feels like to be buried in receipts and spreadsheets when you'd rather be doing the work you love. She knows that most small business owners didn't start their business because they enjoy bookkeeping — and that many feel embarrassed admitting their accounts have fallen behind. She gets it, and there's no judgement.

**Authority:**

- Fellow of the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (FCCA) — the highest membership grade
- BSc (Hons) degree
- Over 20 years' experience in accountancy (since 2002), qualified since 2006
- Experience spanning one-partner practices to Top 50 firms

### 6. FAILURE (Stakes)

What happens if the customer does NOT act:

- Tax deadlines slip — and HMRC penalties start to stack up
- Overpaying tax year after year because nobody is reviewing what you can legitimately claim
- The paperwork pile grows bigger and more expensive to untangle later
- The constant low-level anxiety of knowing your finances aren't in order
- Losing evenings and weekends to admin instead of spending them with family or growing the business

---

|--------|-------|

**Success identity statement:**
You become a business owner who is in control — someone whose finances are handled, whose tax is minimised, and who can focus on what they actually do best.

## PART 6: DESIGN DIRECTION NOTES

---

### Colour Palette Recommendation
- **Primary:** A warm, professional teal or deep blue-green (trustworthy but not corporate)
- **Accent/CTA:** A warm coral, amber, or soft orange (stands out, feels approachable)
- **Neutral base:** Off-white / warm grey backgrounds
- **Text:** Dark charcoal (not pure black — softer on the eyes)

### Typography
- **Headings:** A clean, modern sans-serif with weight — e.g., DM Sans Bold, Plus Jakarta Sans, or Inter Bold
- **Body:** The same family at regular weight, or a highly readable serif for a warmer feel
- **Minimum body size:** 16px, line-height 1.6

### Imagery Direction
- **Hero image:** Relaxed small business owner in a natural setting (café, workshop, small office) — looking confident and at ease. NOT a photo of Emma or a generic office.
- **Guide section:** Emma's professional headshot (updated if possible — current ones are from 2015)
- **Throughout:** Warm, natural light. Real people, not corporate stock. Lifestyle images that reflect the "weight off your shoulders" transformation.
- **Avoid:** Calculators, spreadsheets, piles of paper (clichéd), suited corporate imagery, cold blue tones.

### Navigation
- **Main nav (max 5 items):** Services | About | Testimonials | Contact | **[Book Your Free Consultation]**
- **CTA button in nav:** Contrasting colour, styled as a button (not a text link)
- **Footer:** Contains all secondary pages — FAQs, Articles, Client Portal, Privacy, Socials

### Mobile Priorities
- Tap-to-call phone number
- Sticky CTA button or floating action button on scroll
- Hero text must be fully readable without relying on image overlay
- All sections stack cleanly in single column

## PART 7: LEAD GENERATION FUNNEL NOTES

### Lead Magnet: "7 Tax Deductions Most Small Businesses Miss"

**Format:** PDF checklist (4–6 pages, well-designed)
**Content outline:**
1. Working from home allowance
2. Mileage and travel claims
3. Professional subscriptions and training
4. Equipment and technology (capital allowances)
5. Pension contributions for directors
6. Entertaining vs. hospitality (what counts)
7. The most overlooked claim specific to their business type

**Delivery:** Immediate email delivery on form submission

1. **Email 1 — Deliver the guide:** "Here's your free tax deductions checklist. Here's how to use it."
2. **Email 2 — Problem + Solution:** "The hidden cost of doing your own accounts (and when it makes sense to get help)."
3. **Email 3 — Testimonial story:** Feature I Simpson's story — years of unfiled returns untangled, large rebate secured.
4. **Email 4 — Overcome an objection:** "What does an accountant actually cost? (Less than you think — and less than overpaying tax)."
5. **Email 5 — Paradigm shift:** "The real difference between a sole trader and a limited company — and which one saves you more."
6. **Email 6 — Sales letter:** Summarise the full BrandScript narrative. Clear CTA to book the free consultation.
